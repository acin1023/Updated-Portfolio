Della's Realty Group & Co.

Della‚Äôs Realty provides short term leases in fully furnished luxurious units. Located in the most prominent locations, the company caters to a range of clients from business professionals to vacationing families, by providing comfortable and affordable living to accommodate guests.
In addition, Della‚Äôs Realty Group provides a credit repair service to assists clients who are on the journey to improve their credit scores. The service includes credit report analysis, dispute resolution, debt negotiation, and personalized credit coaching.

Tools Used:

  • Adobe XD
  • Hand Drawn Wireframes
  • TailwindCSS
  • Blobular
  • Fancy Maps
  • Unsplash
  • HTML
  • CSS

Roles Assumed:

  • UX Designer
  • UI Designer
  • Visual Designer
  • Frontend Web Developer

Timeline:

January 2024 - Present

The Goal:

Design a user-friendly website to simplify the process of booking an Airbnb home. In addition, the website needed to incorporate the additional services Della‚Äôs Realty Group offers. The design needed be seamless and easy to use amongst all users, regardless of their technical expertise.
The company also offers a credit repair service and free ebooks to assist users in repairing their credit. The company wanted to provide users with different avenues, all in one place, to help users repair their credit quickly and efficiently as possible.

Design Process:

I met with the company‚Äôs owner to discuss the goals of the company, how a website can help the company achieve it‚Äôs goals, the design layout of the website, and problems users were having booking a home or scheduling a consultation with the credit repair services.

The company's number one goal was to redirect traffic from their social media page to a credible and seamless website. As a person with vague design experience, I wanted to hand draw wireframes to guide me through my journey of creating a functional website. I mapped out individual webpages and added elements for users to easily navigate the website. I designed the website from a user's perspective while also keeping the owner's vision in my designs.

Once the wireframes were mapped out, i met with the owner to get some feedback on what can be improved. A few changes were made to the structure of the website to improve the user's experience. This was then implemented into Adobe XD to visualize the website. Once the structural components of the website were complete, I met with the owner again for feedback. The owner and I were in constant communication in order to bring the company's exact vision of the website to fruition. Some minor changes were made once again and then i was able to proceed to the aesthetic portion of the project.

Keeping the company's aesthetic vision of what the website should look like was challenging. I met with the owner to discuss how certain colors and design choices would make it harder for the user to navigate the website efficiently. One of the problems we discovered was the contrast of the words in comparison to the background made it difficult for readers to read. Another issue, was the color way. Their was no hierarchy for the colors to give importance and significance. We condensed the amount of colors used for the website. Lastly, the major issue was figuring out how much content to put on each page. The initial mockup has so much content that the user may have felt overwhelmed while navigating the website. We were able to come to a resolution and completed the website.

End Result:

Unfortunately the final design was unable to launch for final production. The owner resigned from her position and the company is no longer in business. Though this was not the desired outcome I had envisioned, I am very proud of the end product. I gained so much knowledge and experience working on my first major solo project. I also discovered the areas I can improve upon and I am very eager and excited to learn and develop more skills to advance my career in this field. I am very grateful to have been chosen to work on this design. The owner of the company loved the final design and we are open to collaborating once again on future designs.
